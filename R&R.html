Basic Rules And Regulations:


Mineral Concession Rules:
Mining operations require obtaining mineral concessions or leases from the relevant state or central government authorities. The Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957, and its subsequent amendments govern the grant of mineral concessions.

Environmental Clearances:
Mining projects must obtain environmental clearances under the Environment (Protection) Act, 1986. This involves conducting Environmental Impact Assessments (EIAs) and obtaining approvals from the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change (MoEFCC) or State Pollution Control Boards.

Forest Clearances:
If mining activities are located in forested areas, forest clearances under the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980, are required from the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change (MoEFCC).

Safety Regulations:
Compliance with safety regulations outlined in the Mines Act, 1952, and related rules is mandatory. This includes provisions for safety of workers, mine design, ventilation, use of explosives, and prevention of accidents.

Labour Laws:
Mining operations must adhere to various labour laws and regulations concerning wages, working hours, safety measures, and welfare of mine workers.

Royalty Payments:
Mining lease holders are required to pay royalty to the government based on the quantity and value of minerals extracted.

Community Engagement:
Mining companies are often required to engage with local communities, address their concerns, and contribute to local development under Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) initiatives.

Regulation of Specific Minerals: Different minerals (e.g., coal, iron ore, bauxite, limestone) may have specific regulations governing their mining and transportation. For example, coal mining is governed by the Coal Mines (Special Provisions) Act, 2015.

Health and Environmental Standards: Compliance with health and environmental standards related to water usage, air quality, land reclamation, and waste management is essential.

Regulatory Authorities: The Ministry of Mines at the central level and State Departments of Mines and Geology at the state level are the primary regulatory authorities overseeing mining activities in India.
